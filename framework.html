Target Audience

The Sandbox is open to the following categories of entities:

🏦

Licensed Banks & Financial Institutions (BFIs)

All banks and financial institutions licensed by NRB seeking to test innovative financial products or services.

💳

Licensed Payment-related Institutions

Payment System Operators (PSOs) and Payment Service Providers (PSPs) with existing NRB licensing.

💸

Licensed Remittance Companies

Companies licensed to operate remittance services in Nepal, exploring new digital remittance solutions.

🚀

New FinTech Companies

New FinTech companies incorporated in Nepal that intend to deliver innovative financial products aligned with NRB's objectives.

Eligibility Criteria

Eligibility Criteria may include but will not be limited to the following:

1

Incorporation

The applicant is incorporated in Nepal and is a licensed bank or financial institution, payment system operator, payment service provider, remittance company, or a new FinTech company/firm that intends to deliver new products, services, or solutions that support NRB's objectives as specified in the NRB Act, 2002.

2

Innovation

The proposed product or service must be genuinely innovative or offer a new use of existing technologies, evidenced through market research and comparison of key features against competitors. The product must fall on the eligible products list.

3

Financial Inclusion

The proposed product or service must have the potential to enhance financial inclusion in Nepal. The Applicant must demonstrate this in their application to the extent possible.

4

Need for a Sandbox

The Applicant must demonstrate that live testing is necessary to achieve the testing objective.

5

Readiness for Regulatory Testing

The Applicant must demonstrate technical readiness of their product, service or solution for sandbox testing, along with well-developed testing plans and sufficient safeguards to protect customers. Key risks of the solution and how they can be mitigated must be separately highlighted.

6

Fit & Proper Requirements

The Applicant should be in good standing and all key stakeholders (management, ownership, etc.) should be fit and proper. NRB shall consider the following criteria:

  • The company, including its beneficial owners, BODs, and senior management personnel, are not listed on any blacklist or sanction list issued by a competent authority. A minimum of 3 years must have elapsed since removal if previously listed.
  • The beneficial owners, BODs, and senior management personnel do not have any criminal record.
  • If already licensed, a clear track record of regulatory compliance.
  • At least 2 full-time technical staff (developers and cybersecurity experts).
  • A Project Manager with at least 2 years of relevant experience in fintech/RegTech domain.
7

Consumer Benefit

The Applicant should demonstrate that the proposed products or services offer a benefit to consumers, whether through the product itself or an expanded or more competitive offering.

8

Thematic Eligibility Criteria

Eligible applications should demonstrate that their proposed product, service or solution is suitable for the cohort theme for which applications have been called by NRB. The NRB may prescribe additional eligibility criteria for each cohort at the time of making the application announcement.

9

Complete Application

Eligible applicants must provide complete applications and submit all requested materials. Any errors or omissions will be swiftly addressed in response to NRB inquiries.

Note: Notwithstanding the foregoing, the NRB may, at its discretion, direct or require that certain products, services, or solutions, although permitted under existing regulations, be tested in the Regulatory Sandbox environment to evaluate systemic, operational or consumer protection implications prior to commercial deployment.

Permissible Products, Services, or Solutions

✅ Permissible

  • Application Programming Interfaces (APIs)
  • Mobile money services
  • Retail payments
  • Money transfer services
  • Digital Know Your Customer (KYC)
  • Digital identification services
  • Digital lending
  • Smart contracts
  • Financial inclusion products
  • Cyber security products
  • Embedded finance
  • Regulatory technology products
  • Any other innovation approved by the NRB

❌ Restricted

  • Cryptocurrency
  • Virtual assets
  • Central bank digital currency (CBDC)
  • Online betting, gaming and similar products
  • Any other product or service prohibited under law

Application Requirements

The Sandbox application requirements may include the following information in a form and manner prescribed by the NRB:

1. Eligibility Evidence

A description of how the Applicant has met the Eligibility Criteria described in Annex 1 with supporting evidence.

2. Organizational Details

Full details of the Applicant's organization, including:

  • Governance arrangements, financial standing, technical and business domain expertise
  • Authorized regulatory status if any under the NRB or any other regulator
  • License / registration status
  • Information on key representatives (leadership, senior management, ownership) to satisfy fit & proper requirements
  • Supporting documentation detailing financial standing demonstrating readiness to support live testing

3. Product Details

Full details of the financial product, service, or solution to be experimented on, covering at least:

  • Problem statement that the proposed solution intends to address
  • Benefits, including from the financial inclusion perspective
  • Business model(s), including the target customer and specific use cases with end-to-end illustration
  • Technical architecture, detailing the specific technology and innovative application

4. Partnership Information

In the case of a financial solution, the Applicant must provide detailed information about licensed entity(ies) under discussion for partnership and the stage of such discussion, substantiated with supporting evidence (emails, LOIs, NDAs, MOUs, etc.).

5. Testing Plan

  • Full description of tests to be conducted, including the preferred length of the testing period not exceeding 6 months
  • Targets and key performance indicators (KPIs) to determine the success of test
  • Boundary conditions (target customer types, customer limits, transaction limits, etc.)

6. Regulatory Requirements

  • Details of applicable legal/regulatory requirements and the relaxation being sought
  • Customer safeguards and testing readiness assessment
  • Target customer communications plan including risk disclosures
  • Description of information and cyber security, AML/CFT, consumer protection measures
  • Third-party outsourcing arrangements and due diligence conducted
  • Exit and transition plan for customers at the end of the testing period

Sandbox Process

🔄

Process flow diagram — from application to graduation

1

Cohort Announcement

NRB announces a cohort with a specific innovation theme and opens the application window for approximately 45 working days.

2

Application Submission

Eligible entities submit their application with all required documents through the online portal within the announced application timeline.

3

Initial Screening

Applications are reviewed by the Sandbox Committee for completeness and preliminary eligibility. Shortlisted applications proceed to the Evaluation Team.

4

Evaluation & Selection

The Evaluation Team conducts a thorough assessment, including fit-and-proper checks. Recommended participants are approved by the Sandbox Governing Committee.

5

Sandbox Testing

Accepted participants test their product, service or solution under defined boundary conditions and regulatory oversight. The maximum testing period is 6 months, with a possible one-time extension of up to an additional 6 months.

6

Evaluation & Graduation

Upon completion of testing, NRB evaluates performance against KPIs, consumer protection readiness, and suitability for broader market adoption. Successful participants are graduated through an appropriate exit pathway.

Extension: If a Participant requires an extension, they must communicate in writing to NRB at least 30 calendar days before the end of the agreed testing period. An extension is granted for one time only, up to a maximum of 6 months.
