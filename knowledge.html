Frequently Asked Questions

The NRB Regulatory Sandbox is a cohort-based controlled and supervised testing environment where eligible innovators (called Participants) can test innovative financial products, services, or solutions before their full-scale commercial launch. The Sandbox allows innovators to conduct live testing under defined parameters and regulatory oversight.

A cohort is a structured Sandbox cycle initiated by NRB based on a specific innovation theme. Applications are invited for selected themes within a defined application window and timeline.

NRB has introduced the Sandbox to promote safe and responsible innovation in the financial sector, encourage technology-enabled financial services, deepen financial inclusion, strengthen consumer protection, and support evidence-based financial sector regulation.

The following entities may apply:

  • Licensed Banks and Financial Institutions (BFIs);
  • Licensed Payment System Operators (PSOs);
  • Licensed Payment Service Providers (PSPs);
  • Licensed Remittance Companies; and
  • New FinTech companies incorporated in Nepal.

Yes. New FinTech companies incorporated in Nepal may apply if their proposed product, service, or solution falls within or supports activities under NRB's regulatory purview. Depending on the nature of the innovation, partnership with a licensed financial institution may be required before live testing begins.

Eligible innovations may include:

  • APIs and open banking solutions;
  • Mobile money and retail payment services;
  • Money transfer services;
  • Digital KYC and digital identification services;
  • Digital lending;
  • Smart contracts;
  • Financial inclusion products;
  • Cybersecurity and RegTech solutions;
  • Embedded finance solutions; and
  • Other innovations approved by NRB.

The following are not eligible for Sandbox testing:

  • Cryptocurrency-related products;
  • Virtual assets;
  • Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC)-related products;
  • Online betting and gaming products; and
  • Any activity prohibited by prevailing laws.

Applications can be submitted only when NRB officially announces a cohort and opens the application window. Details regarding themes, timelines, eligibility requirements, and application procedures will be published on the official Sandbox website. Applicants can apply using the "Apply Now" option on the website.

The application window for each cohort will generally remain open for 45 working days.

Applicants can apply using the "Apply Now" option on this website. Along with the application, applicants are required to submit supporting documents within the announced application timeline.

Applicants should submit the documents and information broadly specified in Annex 3 of the "Guidelines on the Regulatory Sandbox" as well as specifically mentioned in the Application Form. See the Application Requirements section for details.

Applicants should generally demonstrate:

  • Incorporation in Nepal;
  • Genuine innovation or novel application of technology;
  • Potential to enhance financial inclusion;
  • Technical readiness for testing;
  • Adequate safeguards and risk mitigation measures;
  • Consumer benefit; and
  • Compliance with fit-and-proper requirements.

The initial testing period may continue for up to 6 months. NRB may grant a one-time extension of up to an additional 6 months if justified. Requests for extension must be submitted in writing at least 30 calendar days before the end of the agreed testing period.

No. Acceptance into or successful graduation from the Sandbox does not automatically guarantee licensing, authorization, or approval for commercial operation. All approvals remain subject to prevailing laws, regulations, and supervisory assessments.

Participants who have successfully tested their product may be graduated through one of the following exit pathways:

  • For licensed entities whose products fall within existing laws, NRB may grant necessary regulatory approval for deployment.
  • For other entities under an existing licensing framework, NRB may consider granting the relevant license, subject to fulfillment of applicable requirements.
  • For licensed entities testing financial solutions, NRB may permit deployment or integration of the tested solution, subject to specific conditions and safeguards.
  • For other entities, NRB may issue a sandbox completion acknowledgement confirming successful conclusion of testing — this does not constitute licensing or regulatory clearance.

The sandbox testing may be deemed successful if the Participant meets the test objectives and other stipulated conditions. NRB shall evaluate: achievement of KPIs and milestones, suitability for broader market adoption in Nepal, risks and corresponding consumer protection measures, and readiness for full licensing or market integration.

However, if the Participant is unable to successfully graduate after evaluation, the Sandbox status will be revoked.

Yes. The NRB reserves the right to revoke any permission where a Participant has:

  • Caused significant customer harm;
  • Materially failed to comply with agreed terms and conditions;
  • Failed to implement appropriate safeguards for consumers;
  • Breached data security or AML/CFT requirements;
  • Submitted misleading information or misrepresented to the NRB;
  • Gone into liquidation or had primary license cancelled; or
  • Failed to meet any other conditions specified by the NRB.

Participants are required to maintain adequate safeguards relating to:

  • Consumer protection;
  • Customer consent and disclosures;
  • Data privacy and cybersecurity;
  • AML/CFT compliance; and
  • Risk management and operational controls.
