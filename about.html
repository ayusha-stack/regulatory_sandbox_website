Introduction

NRB Regulatory Sandbox (hereby referred to as 'Sandbox') is a controlled and supervised testing environment, enabling innovators to test their products, services or solutions prior to their full-scale launch. The Sandbox intends to promote safe and responsible innovations, aiming to deepen financial inclusion efforts through the use of new and innovative technology-enabled products, services or solutions.

The Sandbox also serves as a mechanism allowing regulatory engagement with emerging FinTech innovators, helping both innovators and the central bank better understand risks, strengthen consumer protection, and build market confidence.

Evaluation Team

Based on the theme of the cohort, Evaluation Teams are formulated with members from the Sandbox Committee and other members, including DiFI-Hub members and external experts, if required. The primary responsibility of the Evaluation Team is to solicit and recommend Participants from applications qualified from the Initial Screening.

Objective

The Sandbox aims to achieve the following overarching objectives:

  1. Promote safe and responsible innovation of financial products, services or solutions to enhance access, usage and quality of digital financial services;
  2. Enable innovation of fintech to be deployed and tested in a live environment while ensuring protection of consumers and the financial services sector;
  3. Encourage collaboration between the traditional financial services providers and fintech;
  4. Promote competition and efficiency in the financial services sector while protecting consumers;
  5. Monitor new technologies in the financial sector to ensure appropriate safeguards to manage the risks and contain the consequences of failure;
  6. Provide an avenue for regulatory engagement with firms on new and innovative technologies in the financial services sector; and
  7. Inform financial sector regulation and policy.

Scope

The Sandbox allows financial products, services, or solutions, that NRB can oversee within its legal and regulatory mandate and that facilitate or support financial inclusion and access objectives, to be tested in a time-bound manner under defined boundary conditions.

The Sandbox is intended for FinTech innovators as well as innovators in the financial services sector, including traditional financial services providers. However, acceptance in the Sandbox does not guarantee a license to operate, nor a permission to operate without regulatory oversight or supervision. The Sandbox, therefore, shall not be viewed as a way to circumvent the necessary regulatory approvals.

The Sandbox is a cohort-based mechanism under which applications are called for different themes to foster digital financial services in Nepal. The thematic cohorts are based on the permissible products, services, or solutions, as outlined in Annex 2 of the "Guidelines on the Regulatory Sandbox".

Nepal Rastra Bank considers innovations that enhance financial services, improve consumer experience with financial products or services, promotes competition and efficiency, or enhance financial inclusion. Innovators are expected to comply with consumer protection, data privacy and protection, anti-money laundering and countering of terrorism financing, and cybersecurity requirements.

Benefits

The Sandbox is operationalized to achieve the following benefits:

  • Test products, services, and/or solutions in a controlled environment;
  • Introduce new and innovative technology-enabled products, services, and/or solutions in the financial sector that can deepen financial inclusion efforts;
  • Monitor new technologies in the financial sector to ensure that they do not introduce systemic risk to the financial system;
  • Support in the identification of appropriate consumer protection measures;
  • Expand avenues for regulatory engagement with firms on new and innovative technologies in the financial services space while contributing to economic growth; and
  • Inform regulatory reforms to meet the changing demands of the financial sector.
